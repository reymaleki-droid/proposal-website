Decision Framework

Answer these three questions to narrow your choice.

If you need to be online within 3 weeks, only Package A fits. If you have 5-7 weeks, B becomes viable. For C or E, plan for 3-5 months including design and testing phases.

The timeline is not just about development speed — it also depends on how quickly your team can provide product data, images, and approvals at each checkpoint.

If you plan to grow beyond 200 products, integrate with a CRM, or manage operations from a unified dashboard — WordPress (A/B) will become a limitation. The migration from WordPress to custom code is a rebuild, not an upgrade.

If scalability matters, starting with C and extending to E later saves money compared to starting with A and rebuilding from scratch.

If you want a website that functions as a digital brochure — A or B. If you want it to be a business tool that tracks leads, manages products, and provides operational visibility — C or E.

Package E is the "mature choice" because it treats the website, CRM, bot, and dashboard as parts of a single system. Data flows consistently. Everyone uses the same source of truth.

Our Recommendation

Based on your product range (IT equipment, networking, CCTV), team size, and growth ambitions, Package E provides the strongest foundation. It eliminates class-of-problems rather than solving them individually:

  • No WordPress maintenance burden or security surface
  • CRM integration means inquiries become trackable leads, not lost emails
  • Telegram bot means your sales team manages products from anywhere
  • Unified dashboard means the Owner/GM has real-time visibility
  • Data integrity layer means no conflicting records across systems
  • Extending from C to E later costs 30-40% on top — starting with E costs nothing extra

If budget or timeline constraints make E impractical right now, start with C. It shares the same architecture, and upgrading from C to E is an extension, not a rebuild.
